Review requests are the engine of your Rayt program. Get the timing and copy right and you'll see response rates of 15–25% — enough to keep every product page fresh with recent reviews.

## Choosing the right delay

Timing matters more than any other variable. For apparel and hard goods, send the first request 10–14 days after delivery — long enough for customers to actually use the product, short enough that they still remember ordering. For consumables, 5–7 days works better.

Rayt lets you set a delay per product category so you don't have to compromise. Head to **Campaigns → Timing** and tweak each category independently.

## Writing the email

Short beats clever. Your subject line should name the product ("How's your new kettle?") rather than sell the review ("Please leave a review!"). Inside, lead with a single star-rating widget — customers who click it are taken to the full review form on your site, where completion rates jump.

Keep the email to three sentences of copy plus the rating widget. Anything longer dilutes the ask.

## Testing before launch

Send yourself a preview from every template before enabling the campaign. Check it renders properly in Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail — those three cover roughly 85% of consumer email clients. If any of them look broken, tweak the template or reach out to support for help.

Once the campaign is live, watch your send volume and open rate for the first week. If opens dip below 30%, try a new subject line; if clicks are low, shorten the copy.

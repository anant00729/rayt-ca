Incentives can boost review volume by 2–3x, but used clumsily they erode trust or land you in trouble with platform guidelines. Here's how to do them well in Rayt.

## Choosing a reward

The best incentive is small, relevant, and applied to the customer's next order — typically a 10% or 15% discount code valid for 30 days. Avoid cash-equivalent rewards (Amazon cards, PayPal transfers) because they signal "paid review" and violate Google Shopping review-feed rules.

A small discount reads as a thank-you, not a bribe. That's the line you want to stay on.

## Configuring in Rayt

Open **Campaigns → Incentives** and create a new reward. You'll set the discount percentage, expiry window, and minimum rating required (we recommend any rating — asking only for 4 or 5 stars is explicitly against most platform guidelines and will get you delisted).

Rayt will auto-generate unique discount codes per reviewer so they can't be shared in coupon aggregator sites.

## Disclosure

You must disclose that a review was submitted after receiving an incentive. Rayt handles this automatically — reviews collected through an incentive campaign get a small "Incentivized" badge attached. This keeps you compliant with FTC, Google, and Meta rules without extra work on your end.

If you're syndicating reviews to Google Shopping, be aware that incentivized reviews are excluded from the feed. That's Google's rule, not ours — plan accordingly when forecasting your Shopping ad review counts.

A public reply turns a one-way review into a conversation. Replying well — especially to critical reviews — is one of the highest-leverage things you can do on Rayt. This guide covers tone, timing, and process.

## Where to reply

Open **Reviews → All** and click any review to expand it. The reply composer sits at the bottom of the expanded view. Anything you write here is shown publicly under the review on your product pages and syndicated with the review to Google Shopping.

Private notes — for internal context — live in a separate tab and are never shown publicly.

## Replying to positive reviews

Don't default to "Thanks!" — it reads as boilerplate. Instead, echo back something specific the reviewer mentioned ("Glad the oak finish matched your table — that's a tricky stain to get right"). Two sentences max. You'll come across as a real person, not a bot.

Aim to reply to around 30% of 5-star reviews. Replying to all of them dilutes the signal; replying to none feels cold.

## Replying to critical reviews

Reply to every review under 3 stars — ideally within 48 hours. Acknowledge the specific problem, apologize without being performative, and offer a concrete path forward (a replacement, a refund, or a direct contact email). Keep it short; long replies read as defensive.

Negative reviews that get a thoughtful public reply have a measurable positive effect on future buyers. Data from our customers shows a 10–15% conversion uplift on products where critical reviews are consistently answered.

Once you're collecting reviews at scale, the firehose view stops being useful. Rayt's filters let you slice reviews by rating, product, sentiment, and keyword so you can focus on exactly what you need to triage.

## The filter bar

Every view in **Reviews → All** has a filter bar at the top. The most useful combinations, in our experience:

- **Rating ≤ 3** — every critical review, newest first
- **Unreplied + Rating ≤ 3** — your moderation queue
- **Contains "refund" OR "broken"** — product-quality signals
- **Last 7 days + Photo attached** — great candidates for your site-wide carousel

Filters stack, so you can combine any of the above with a product filter to focus on a single SKU.

## Saved views

Any filter combination can be saved as a named view via the "Save view" button. Saved views appear in the left sidebar and are shared with everyone on your team, so you can set up standardized queues ("Moderation", "VIP", "Media-rich") that anyone can pick up.

Views update live — you don't need to re-run the filter when new reviews arrive.

## Keyboard shortcuts

Power users: `/` focuses the search box, `j`/`k` moves between reviews, `r` opens the reply composer, and `e` marks the current review as reviewed. A full list is available under **?** when you're signed in.

If you spend more than an hour a day in Rayt, learning these four shortcuts will save you several hours a week.
